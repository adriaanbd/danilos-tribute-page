Danilo Perez

A musician, an educator and a humanitarian
Danilo Perez walking with drummer Brian Blade and bassist John Patitucci in Boston, Massachussetts, United States of America

    Some biographical facts of Danilo:

  • 1965 - Born in Panama City, Panama
  • 1968 - Begun musical studies with his father
  • 1975 - Was studying European classical piano repertoire at the National Conservatory in Panama
  • 1985 - Was awarded a Fulbright USA Scholarship to study in the United States
  • 1989 - Became youngest member to be appointed in Dizzy Gillespie's United Nations Orchestra
  • 1993 - Releases his first album, Danilo Perez
  • 1994 - Releases his second album, The Journey, which makes it to top ten jazz lists of New York’s Village Voice, the New York Times, Billboard Magazine, and the Boston Globe
  • 1995 - Appointed to the faculty of the New England Conservatory
  • 1995 - Releases his third album, Panamonk, a tribute to Thelonius Monk, which DownBeat named “One of the most important jazz piano albums in the history”
  • 1998 - his album Central Avenue, placed mejoranera music (a style of Panamanian folklore singing) within a contemporary jazz context and earned Grammy and Latin Grammy nominations
  • 2003 - Founded the Panama Jazz Festival
  • 2005 - Founded the Danilo Perez Foundation which brings art and music to children living in communities of extreme poverty throughout the Republic of Panama

"[...] to not think about music just in musical terms but as a tool for the betterment of society. It's about creating music that brings people together."

-- Danilo Perez
